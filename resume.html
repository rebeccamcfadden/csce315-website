AI Platform Explore Intern, Microsoft

During the summer of 2019, I worked as an Explore Intern with Microsoft's Azure AI Platform team. I collaborated with two other interns to develop documentation for deploying Kubeflow on Azure. Kubeflow is an open-source Kubernetes toolkit, originally developed by Google, for containerizing ML models. My team and I built sample pipelines to showcase how the Azure ML Python SDK could be utilized to train, register, and deploy ML models using Kubeflow on Azure. We created and helped to launch the official Kubeflow documentation page for deploying on Azure. In addition, we were invited to and spoke at Google's Kubeflow Doc Sprint, where we contributed to the redesign of their landing page.

Student Researcher, Texas A&M Interface Ecology Lab

In the spring of my freshman year, I joined the Interface Ecology Lab at Texas A&M as a web developer and student researcher. Our focus was developing LiveMache: a collaborative ideation platform. LiveMache is used in some Texas A&M and other universities’ classes to support co-creation and interactive design projects. It is infinitely zoomable and allows dragging and dropping many types of media, such as videos, photos, PDFs, and even video chat. I worked to implement new features and bug fixes in HTML, CSS, and Javascript. Most recently, I worked on overhauling the touch interactions and implementing pinch zooming capabilities.

Explainer, National Air and Space Museum

My junior year of high school, I began a volunteer position at the National Air and Space museum in Washington D.C. as an Explainer.
